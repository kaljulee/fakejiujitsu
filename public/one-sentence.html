Introduction Fake Jiu Jitsu in One Sentence

In one informal sentence, a Fake Jiu Jitsu is:

An approach to training jiu jitsu that trades the concepts of 'technique' and 'position' for a small set of 1st order principles, and the higher-order principles derived from them.

This definition is in contrast to Brazilian Jiu Jitsu which can be described as

A set of positions (top and bottom) and techniques (offense and defense) learned from a teacher, practiced by a student.

There is very little room to talk about BJJ without referring to technique and position; it would arguably be incoherent to do so.

If you are performing BJJ, you are almost certainly attempting to execute a technique from a position. Further, these techniques and positions are ususally handed down from a teacher, fully formed.


I argue that the concepts of position and technique are not actually fundamental, but instead are just one way of slicing up grappling into words.

Fake Jiu Jitsus are alternative ways to talk about grappling, which may or may not include these concepts.

This is a claim about how grappling works, and what is really going on:

  • when we wrestle around in pajamas
  • when we talk about wrestling around in pajamas

FJJ2 is a specific model developed by the Nice Train Lab.

(I have an undergraduate degree in philosophy, so I know how to use confusing terminology responsibly)

A single-sentence summary of FJJ2:

Using Turtle Space Control to maintain your ability to apply alternating Shotput-powered Bridge and Shrimp movements onto an opponent you are Connected to, Timed contrary to an established Pattern.

The bold terms are the model’s 1st-order principles.

The statements in FJJ2 are not meant to necessarily be accurate descriptions of the world. The intention is to have a grappling model that makes testable predictions.

Once I have my model and predictions, I can

  • run experiments
  • derive 2nd and 3rd order principles
  • note apparent deficiencies in the model
  • eventually revise the model

So, to recap my definitions:

  • BJJ is a real martial art defined around positions and techniques
  • Fake jiu jitus are alternative ways of describing and training grappling
  • FJJ2 is a specific model and prediction set

Introduction Fake Jiu Jitsu in One Sentence

In one informal sentence, Fake Jiu Jitsu is:

An approach to training jiu jitsu that trades the concepts of 'technique' and 'position' for a small set of 1st order principles, and the higher-order principles derived from them, verified through first-person experience.

This definition is in contrast to Brazilian Jiu Jitsu, which can be described as

A set of positions (top and bottom) and techniques (offense and defense) learned from a teacher, practiced by a student.

There is very little room to talk about BJJ without referring to technique and position; they are fundamental to the language of BJJ.

Defined through its 1st-order principles, FJJ is

Using Turtle Space Control to maintain your ability to apply alternating Shotput-powered Bridge and Shrimp movements onto an opponent you are Connected to, Timed contrary to an established Pattern.

Throughout this document, I'll be referring to Fake Jiu Jitsu as FJJ or FJJ2. FJJ2 is the latest version of FJJ, but in general the terms can be used interchangeably.
